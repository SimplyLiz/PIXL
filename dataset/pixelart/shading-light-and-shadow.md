# Light and Shadow in Pixel Art

By Raymond Schlitter (SLYNYRD)

## Core Principle

"Light is what lets us see the world and affects the way we see color. Color is the way objects emit light." In pixel work, this translates to managing color clusters to simulate shadows, highlights, and mid-tones.

## Light Source Establishment

Establish a light source early in any illustration. "This will dictate the flow of shadows, tell you where highlights should be placed, and otherwise guide you."

Natural overhead lighting (mimicking sun position) feels intuitive, though top-right or top-left placement creates dynamic contrast while maintaining balance.

## Types of Light and Shadow

### Direct Light

The primary light source. Creates the brightest highlights where surfaces face the light directly.

### Ambient Light

Indirect light bouncing from the environment. Fills shadows partially, preventing pure black. In outdoor scenes, sky provides blue-tinted ambient light.

### Cast Shadow

Occurs when an object blocks light, creating a shadow on another surface. The umbra is the darkest part where light is fully blocked.

### Form Shadow

The gradual transition from light to shadow across a curved surface. Shows the object's three-dimensional volume.

### Volume Shadow

Self-projected shadow resulting from the object's own form blocking light. The most common shadow type in sprite work.

## Mood Through Lighting

Lighting dramatically influences emotional impact:

- **Hazy conditions**: Soft, diffuse shadows
- **Clear atmosphere**: Crisp, well-defined shadows
- **Space/vacuum**: Elongated, razor-sharp shadows (no atmospheric scattering)
- **Underlighting**: Creates unnatural, ominous mood
- **Rim lighting**: Highlights edges, creates dramatic separation from background

## Shading Approaches

### Cel Shading

Hard edges between light and shadow values — no blending. The boundary between light and dark is crisp. Works well at small pixel scales where gradient transitions aren't possible anyway.

### Soft Shading

Gradual transitions using multiple color steps. Better for larger sprites with more palette room.

## Common Mistakes

### Pillow Shading

Shading that follows the outline shape rather than the light source. Creates a puffy, flat appearance. **Always shade based on light direction, not sprite outline.**

### No Light Source

Trying to shade an object with no clear light direction creates generic, unconvincing results. Pick a direction and commit.

## Philosophy

"The simple approach always works well" in pixel art lighting, though "execution should be handled with care." Success depends on finding appropriate contrast levels and shadow treatment to create visual depth and immersion.
